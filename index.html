Interactive Basic Calcium Dynamics Simulator

This interactive simulator runs a repeating 2-second sweep simulating presynaptic Ca2+ dynamics in a basic well-mixed single compartment model. One can use this to explore how different components of Calcium homeostasis such as Ca2+ extrusion or buffering parameters, and factors such as exogenous Calcium buffers/dyes influence free Ca2+ transients.

Controls let you adjust:

  • Number & frequency of Ca2+ pulses (each 3000 µM/s amplitude)
  • Pulse duration (0–10 ms), endogenous buffer type (fast/slow), optional dye (OGB-1)
  • Buffer concentration & extrusion rate

The top chart shows free Ca2+, the bottom chart shows bound buffers (endogenous + dye). Given that the bound buffer concentration is a close reflection of Calcium dynamics measured by optical imaging methods it is important to understand the circumstances where fluorescence fails to replicate the free Ca2+ concentration.
